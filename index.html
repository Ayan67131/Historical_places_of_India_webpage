Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

A white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

agra fort

One of the oldest historical places in India is the Agra Fort, located in Uttar Pradesh. A true example of the stunning Mughal form of architecture, the 16th century Agra Fort was built during Akbar's reign and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site as well.

Mysore Palace

Boasting of the grand Indo-Saracenic style of architecture, the Mysore Palace, located in the city of Mysore in Karnataka, remains one of the most popular historical sites in the country. Complete with opulent domes, gardens, arches, turrets as well as colonnades, the palace was the former residence of the royal family of Mysore

Khajuraho Temples

The famous Khajuraho Temples in Madhya Pradesh are amongst the top 10 historical places in India, popular for being home to a total of 85 Hindu and Jain temples, spread across three complexes. It is here where you can see intricate rock carvings portraying dance, music, passion and eroticism, in addition to learning about the rich culture of the region during the medieval period.

Meenakshi Amman Temple

Built in the Dravidian style of architecture, the Meenakshi Amman Temple ranks as one of the most beautiful ancient places in India. Built in the early 17th century, the temple is located on the southern banks of the Vaigai river in Madurai, Tamil Nadu. Also known as the Sri Meenakshi Temple, this temple is primarily dedicated to Goddess Parvati and Lord Shiva.

Golden Temple

The top 10 historical places in India also include the world-famous Golden Temple, located in the city of Amritsar in Punjab. Also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, the Golden temple is the most important religious site for the Sikhs, and celebrates humanity, tranquillity and brotherhood.

Elephanta Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Elephanta Caves, located on the Gharapuri Island around 11 kilometres from Mumbai, is another one of the top historical places in India. Considered to be one of the most beautiful specimens of rock-cut art and architecture, dating back to the medieval times, these caves today are merely the remnants of what used to be elaborate artworks.

Mehrangarh Fort

Also known as Mehran Fort, the Mehrangarh Fort in Jaipur is one of the most famous ancient places in India. Built by Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur, in 1459, the fort is also one of the largest in the country, and is situated atop a hill, at an elevation of over 400 feet.

Charminar

Amongst the most famous historical places in India, Hyderabad's Charminar is a mosque that dates back to the 16th century. This monument has been made in the Indo-Islamic styles of architecture, and has subtle Persian influences in it. Complete with minarets, domes and arches, Charminar also boasts of beautiful stucco work.

Sanchi Stupa

Amongst the top historical places in India, the Sanchi Stupa dates back to the 3rd century, and is said to be one of the oldest monuments in the country. Constructed in the Buddhist style of architecture, the Sanchi Stupa is also an important Buddhist pilgrimage site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ajanta & Ellora Caves

n addition to being UNESCO World Heritage Sites, the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra are also the top historical places in India. Complete with beautiful sculptures, paintings as well as frescoes, Buddhist monasteries, Hindu and Jain temples, these caves are said to bear testimony of some of the earliest forms of Buddhist architecture. While the Ajanta Caves are 29 in number and date back to between the 2nd and 6th centuries, the Ellora Caves are more extravagant and are 34 in number, and date back to between the 6th and 11th centuries.

India Gate

Of all the famous historical places in India, India Gate possibly ranks at the top. An iconic landmark of Delhi, this sandstone-granite arch gate is amongst the largest war memorials in all of India, and is surrounded by verdant lawns and gardens. Also known as the All-India War Memorial, the India Gate is located along the Rajpath, and is often said to be similar to the Arc de Triomphe in France, the Gateway of India in Mumbai and the Arch of Constantine in Rome. With a height of 42 metres, this memorial has been dedicated to the 82,000 Indian and British soldiers who sacrificed their lives in the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.
